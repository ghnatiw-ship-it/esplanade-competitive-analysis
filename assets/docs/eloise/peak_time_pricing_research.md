# Eloise — OpenTable Peak-Time Experience Research

> Date: April 13, 2026
> Question: are restaurants using OpenTable to force a higher-spend dining format into peak inventory?

## Short Answer

Yes.

The behavior exists now on OpenTable, but it is almost never described as a `minimum spend`.

Instead, restaurants use:

- tasting menus
- chef's experiences
- family-style menus
- room-specific premium menus
- group menus with whole-table participation

That is the correct framing for Eloise too.

## What OpenTable Supports

OpenTable Experiences supports:

- experience-specific booking types
- fixed pricing per guest
- prepaid or pay-at-restaurant formats
- date and time restriction
- partial inventory allocation
- experience-specific descriptions and imagery

## What This Means For Eloise

The idea is valid, but the packaging matters.

Bad framing:

- minimum spend required
- mandatory spend floor
- premium seating charge

Good framing:

- `Taste Eloise`
- `Taste Eloise Signature`
- `Prime Time Experience`
- `Chef's Selection`

The experience should feel like:

- a more complete way to enjoy Eloise
- a curated version of the restaurant
- a premium reservation type for prime inventory

It should not feel like:

- a penalty for booking at the good time
- a bundle discount
- a hidden surcharge

## Best Structural Models For Eloise

### Model 1: Peak-time dinner experience

- Thu-Sat
- `6:30`, `7:00`, `7:30`
- whole-table participation
- 4-course curated menu

Best for:

- protecting the highest-demand two-tops and four-tops

### Model 2: Group trigger

- only for `4+`, `6+`, or `8+`
- family-style or chef's menu

Best for:

- making larger prime tables produce a stronger and more predictable cheque

### Model 3: Seat-type trigger

- chef's counter or best tables require the premium menu

Best for:

- a softer launch if Eloise wants less guest friction at first

## Recommended Direction

For Eloise, the best first test is Model 1.

That means:

- keep normal dinner available before and after the peak window
- use an OpenTable Experience only for the best-performing slots
- price the experience to land the target cheque before alcohol
- use pairings and supplements for upside

## Key Risk

The concept will fail if it is introduced as a revenue rule instead of a dining format.

That is why the language, menu design, and host script matter as much as the price.
